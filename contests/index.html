STREET SAFETY CHALLENGE

Scared of data? Contact us!

Participants in the Street Safety Challenge are encouraged to engage with open, public data about street safety and use their talents and skills in data visualization, art, design, and storytelling to inform public policy making. Open, public data on traffic accidents involving cars, pedestrians, and cyclists that occurred in the City of Cambridge from 2010 to 2013 are made available by the City of Cambridge, Massachusetts.

Projects might address:

  • Where are unsafe intersections in Cambridge?
  • What are safe routes for cyclists with low levels of comfort riding a bicycle?
  • Where is it safe for children to bike or walk to schools?
  • Have a public safety, policy-relevant question you hope to address? Add it here!

CHALLENGE GUIDELINES

WED, DEC 31 – Deadline to submit for award consideration
FRI, JAN 9; 630 to 9 pm – Workshop open to the public, all participants, and stakeholders at Microsoft NERD Center. RSVPs are encouraged here.

Submissions received by the deadline will be juried based on two criteria, whether the submission creatively applies the supplied open data and whether it informs a policy discussion around public safety in Cambridge, MA. Awards for outstanding submissions include: cash prizes, a potential fellowship to continue development of a web application, and public installations of submissions. A final workshop will bring together all contest participants to develop insights from data and generate policy impact.

Challenge judges include:
Nadeem Mazen City Council Member, City of Cambridge, MA
Holly St. Clair Director of Data Services Massachusetts Area Planning Council
Nidhi Subbaraman Staff Writer at BetaBoston of the Boston Globe
