Welcome to the official website of the Open Data Discourse!

WHAT IS IT?

The Open Data Discourse: Public Safety Challenge is a student-led initiative. The mission of the Open Data Discourse: Public Safety Challenge is to provide a purpose, a motivation, and incentives for citizens to engage with data on the Cambridge City Council Open Data Portal. To accomplish this, the DUSP Data Challenge will host a month-long contest for MIT students and other citizens to use their talents and skills (in data visualization, performance art, design, story-telling etc.) to respond to a policy question and inform future public policy.

HOW IT WORKS

  1. Your elected officials posts issues and questions they have about the policy discussions they are having right now, asking for your feedback.
  2. You will be provided with some information related to this policy question and have about one-month to respond.
  3. When you participate, your policy maker agrees to sit face-to-face and discuss your contribution and how they can use it to reform policies.


BENEFITS

The Open Data Discourse: Public Safety Challenge the Cambridge City Council outsource creative policy solutions, make data-driven decisions, and find efficiencies in operation. It deepens and broadens their engagement with citizens, brings much needed publicity to its new Open Data Portal, and helps improve the quality of municipal services.

At the same time, the Open Data Discourse: Public Safety Challenge gives participants an avenue to more meaningfully participate in their democracy, utilizing the talent and skills they come with to inform and improve policy. By participating, citizens will gain experience working with data, learn new skills, and develop projects that could contribute to their own ongoing work.
